Frequently Asked Questions

Information about academics, admissions, and campus life at the Rabbinical College of Telshe.

The Yeshiva is located on a sprawling 57-acre campus at 28400 Euclid Avenue in Wickliffe, Ohio, a suburb of Cleveland. The serene, academic environment provides an ideal Makom Torah for bochurim.

Telshe Yeshiva offers a comprehensive theological and academic curriculum including the Mechina (High School for grades 9-12), undergraduate and graduate Rabbinical programs in the Bais Medrash, and advanced study in the Kollel.

The "Telzer Derech" refers to the Yeshiva's historic, rigorous approach to Talmudic study established in Lithuania. It emphasizes deep inductive reasoning, precise logic (Higayon), clear conceptual understanding (Havanah), and intense character development through Mussar.

You can support the Yeshiva and elevate a Neshama through our Memorial page. Options include sponsoring a day of learning, arranging for Kaddish to be recited by a Talmid Chacham during the year of mourning, or dedicating a permanent memorial plaque on campus.

Yes, prospective talmidim and their parents are encouraged to visit our Makom Torah. Please contact the Yeshiva office at (440) 943-5300 or email info@telsheyeshiva.edu to arrange a visit and schedule a farher (admissions interview) with the Hanholah.
