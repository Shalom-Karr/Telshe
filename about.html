Our History & Mission

From the glorious Botei Medrash of pre-war Lita to our sprawling campus in Wickliffe, Ohio.

The Origins in Telz

Yeshivas Telshe, commonly known as Telshe Yeshiva, traces its roots back to 1875 in the town of Telšiai (Telz), Lithuania. Under the leadership of early Gedolei Yisroel such as Rabbi Eliezer Gordon and Rabbi Yosef Leib Bloch, the institution grew into one of the largest and most prestigious centers of Torah study in Eastern Europe, pioneering the "Telzer Derech"—a highly analytical and systematic approach to Limud Hatorah.

Miraculous Rebirth in America

During the tragic events of World War II, the original yeshiva was destroyed by the Nazis. However, the legacy was not lost. In 1940, two senior faculty members, Rabbi Eliyahu Meir Bloch and Rabbi Chaim Mordechai Katz, had traveled to the United States on a fundraising mission. Unable to return to their families or their students in Europe, they resolved to ensure the survival of the yeshiva.

In 1941, they formally reopened Yeshivas Telshe in Cleveland, Ohio. Starting with just a handful of talmidim in a small building, their profound Mesiras Nefesh allowed the Yeshiva to rapidly expand and re-establish itself as a pillar of American Torah Jewry.

The Wickliffe Campus & Today

By 1957, the yeshiva had outgrown its original Cleveland facilities and relocated to its current sprawling, 57-acre campus in Wickliffe, Ohio. Today, Telshe Yeshiva features a vibrant Makom Torah including the Mechina, Bais Medrash, and Kollel.

The Yeshiva continues to produce generations of Talmidei Chachamim, Rabbonim, and Manhigim who carry forward the timeless traditions and academic rigor of the original European institution.
